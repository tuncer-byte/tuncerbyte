OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic engineers wrote these guides for their own teams. All of them are publicly available.

Let's go through each one.

---

## 01 — OpenAI: AI in the Enterprise

A practical framework for integrating AI into enterprise environments. Covers security standards, access management, and operational scaling strategies — OpenAI's official playbook for large organizations.

If you're planning large-scale deployments, this is where to start.

---

## 02 — OpenAI: Building Agents

A step-by-step guide to building autonomous agents. Tool use mechanics, decision loops, and the fundamental building blocks of agent architecture — all in one place.

Particularly useful for understanding **multi-step reasoning** and **tool call** flows.

---

## 03 — Google: Prompting Guide 101

A comprehensive prompt engineering handbook prepared for Gemini and Google Workspace. Structured from beginner to advanced, covering zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought techniques — with clear guidance on when each approach works.

---

## 04 — OpenAI: Scaling AI Use Cases

How do you identify, prioritize, and successfully scale AI use cases across an organization? OpenAI shares its methodology here.

A solid resource for closing the gap between AI strategy and execution.

---

## 05 — Google: 601 Real-World Use Cases

601 generative AI scenarios collected by Google from large organizations worldwide. Concrete business outcomes, real workflows, and evidence of where AI actually delivers value.

A strong reference whenever someone asks "but what's the actual ROI?"

---

## 06 — Kaggle: Agent Companion

Prepared in collaboration between Kaggle and Google, this guide documents engineering standards for agent architecture and memory management.

Technically dense. Recommended for anyone going deep on context management and stateful agent design.

---

## 07 — Google: Prompt Engineering Whitepaper

Frameworks for extracting maximum performance from language models, backed by Google's research team. Turns prompt design into a systematic engineering discipline rather than a guessing game.

---

## 08 — Anthropic: Claude Academy & MCP

Technical training on **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** — the protocol that connects Claude models to external tools and systems. Anthropic Academy's MCP and agent capability modules are the practical entry point here.

[Read our detailed post on Anthropic Academy.](/en/blog/anthropic-academy-free-courses)

---

## 09 — Anthropic: Model Specification

The technical document explaining how Claude thinks, its values, and decision-making mechanisms. Anthropic's most comprehensive public document for understanding model behavior.

Essential reading for engineers building AI systems that need to interact with or rely on Claude in production.

---

Reading these 9 guides together gives you engineering perspectives from all three major AI companies:

- **OpenAI** → practical agents and enterprise scaling
- **Google** → prompt engineering and real-world scenarios
- **Anthropic** → model architecture and modern integration

All free. All public.
